Computer Vision - Controlled Electrical Stimulator for People Suffering from Paralysis

In the summer of 2018, I worked with computer vision for the first time. I designed a computer vision - based controller for a device that performs Functional Electrical Stimulation (FES). FES is the process of applying electrical stimulation to the affected muscles of patients suffering from paralysis while they imagine themselves performing an action such as grasping an object. The stimulation allows them to effect the action, and repeated sessions have been shown to help patients regain some of their ability to independently perform these actions. My project was to use computer vision to determine how close a patients' hand was to an object they are trying to grasp, and trigger the electrical stimulations automatically. This could potentially allow patients to perform these exercises at home, since the stimulations are typically controlled by a physiotherapist at a clinic.
